http://www.deltamedicalcentre.org Private clinic in Maun. Slogan: "We Treat, God Heals". Nice lobby: air-conditioned and with soft couches. Doctor (Dr. Hoko) educated in Germany. Prices as high in Switzerland, e.g. flushing of an eye to remove foreign body: P571 (100 CHF). Unclear if the body actually gets removed.

http://www.cheetahpark.com Cheetah conservation park, Namibia.

http://www.swakoplodge.com Hotel/hostel/bar/restaurant in Swakopmund. Unclear if it is called "Hotel zum Grünen Kranz" or "Swakop Lodge".

http://www.alter-action.info Sandboarding on the dunes next to Swakopmund, Namibia. The 4h activity (9.30–13.30) includes the ride to the dunes, snowboards (for standup boarding) and ?boards (for lay-down boarding), about 5–6 stand-up rides (depends on how quickly you manage to climb up again as there is no lift), 2–3 lay-down rides (approx. 70 km/h), sandwiches/coke/beer, ride back to Swakopmund, and a DVD (one person is filming all the time, later you can buy 1MP still images for N$20 each from him as well). There are T-shirts and hoodies for sale (N$100 and N$250, resp.), but an XL-size hoodie feels more like S.

http://www.swakopmund-museum.de Museum in Swakopmund with many different things on display: stuffed animals, horns and skeletons of animals and birds, overview of Namibian people (Nama, Damara, Herero, Himba, San, etc.) and their culture, Rössing uranium mine, German culture in Namibia, etc. etc.
